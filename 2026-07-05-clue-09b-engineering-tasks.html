🔊 Optional Challenge 2 — The Science of Sound Zones
Inside this building's Mega Labs, multiple classes can run simultaneously — without walls or dividers — thanks to directional (parametric) speaker technology. Conventional speakers spread sound in all directions, like a light bulb illuminating a whole room. Directional speakers behave more like a laser: they fire a narrow beam of ultrasonic waves through the air. As those waves travel, they interact with the air itself to produce audible sound — but only within the beam. Step outside the zone and the sound vanishes. This acoustic engineering breakthrough means the Mega Labs can flip from separate, isolated classrooms to a single giant teaching space with just a software switch — no construction required.

💭 As a group, discuss: Where else could directional speaker technology change the world? Think hospitals, airports, public spaces, or even your own school. What engineering problems would you solve with it?
