The Riddle — read aloud at the starting point

I'm not alive, but I stand my ground,
A large brown guardian, campus-renowned.
I never growl, I never roar,
But plenty have stopped and stared in awe.

From where you stand, five doors you'll see
But only one will lead to me.
You'll find me just inside the door,
When someone shouts — look no more!

Don't leave a straggler out in the cold —
I'd snack on the slow ones, or so I've been told!

Find me in this place of learning,
Strike my pose — careful, no squirming!
