Welcome to the Eoin Wilkinson Collection! 📚

This display section is named in honour of Eoin H. Wilkinson, who served as a librarian at Macquarie University from 1971 to 1987. Located just inside the library entrance on the right, the section hosts curated art and cultural displays for the campus community. Today it features a stunning collection of blown glass works — each one unique, handcrafted, and alive with colour.

🔥 Optional Challenge 2 — The Art of Glass
Glass blowing is nearly 2,000 years old, invented in ancient Syria around 50 BCE. A glassblower gathers molten glass — heated to over 1,000°C — on a hollow metal blowpipe, then inflates it like a balloon by blowing air through the tube. The glass is shaped using hand tools, gravity, and centrifugal force while being constantly reheated in a furnace. The vivid colours you see in the display come from metal oxides fused into the glass: cobalt makes blue, copper makes green, and tiny amounts of gold produce deep ruby red. Despite its liquid-like appearance when molten, glass is technically an amorphous solid — its atoms are arranged randomly, unlike the orderly crystals in ice or metal.
