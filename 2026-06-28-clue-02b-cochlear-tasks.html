🔬 Optional Challenge 2 — STEM
The cochlear implant was pioneered by Australian researcher Professor Graeme Clark in the 1970s. Unlike a hearing aid (which amplifies sound), an implant works by converting sound into electrical signals that are sent directly to the auditory nerve, bypassing damaged hair cells in the cochlea. Today, Cochlear implants have restored hearing to over 700,000 people worldwide.
