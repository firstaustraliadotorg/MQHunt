Head to the Biology Discovery Centre — it's in the diagonally opposite corner of the building where you found Frank the Bear. Walk around to the far side and look for the entrance.

Inside you'll find Jack — a Tarbosaurus bataar skeleton, a close cousin of T-Rex, cast from a fossil found in Mongolia's Gobi Desert approximately 75 million years ago. Named after Professor Jack Bassett, he's been standing guard here since 1996. At 6.5 metres long, he was still just a teenager when he died!

🔬 Optional Challenge 2 — STEM
Jack isn't made of real bone — he's a cast, made from moulds taken from the original fossil. Palaeontologists do this to protect fragile fossils while still sharing them with the world. Tarbosaurus bataar lived during the Late Cretaceous period (~75 million years ago) in what is now Mongolia. Despite being nearly as large as T-Rex, scientists know Jack was still growing — his unfused bone growth plates reveal he was a juvenile.

💭 As a group, discuss: How do scientists determine the age of a dinosaur skeleton? What is the difference between a fossil cast and a real fossil, and why does it matter?
