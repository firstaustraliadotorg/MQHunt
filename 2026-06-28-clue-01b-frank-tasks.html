🔬 Optional Challenge 2 — STEM
Frank was a Kodiak bear (Ursus arctos middendorffi) — the largest subspecies of brown bear on Earth, capable of weighing over 600 kg. After Frank died at Taronga Zoo in 1978, his body was prepared through taxidermy: his skin was removed, cleaned, and pulled over a wooden frame padded with cotton wool — a process that took nine months to complete.

💭 As a group, discuss: What is taxidermy, and what scientific purposes does it serve? Why might a university keep a taxidermied animal on display rather than a photograph?
