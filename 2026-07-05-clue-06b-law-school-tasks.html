Welcome to the Law School! ⚖️

You're standing in one of Australia's most remarkable university buildings — the Michael Kirby Building, home of the Macquarie Law School. Named after Justice Michael Kirby, one of Australia's most celebrated High Court judges and a tireless champion of human rights, this building was designed by architects Hassell and completed in 2024. The entire four-storey structure is built from cross-laminated timber (CLT) — a sustainable, carbon-storing alternative to concrete and steel.

🌿 Optional Challenge 2 — STEM & Sustainability
The Michael Kirby Building is built almost entirely from Cross-Laminated Timber (CLT) — engineered panels made by gluing layers of solid timber at 90° angles to each other. This makes the panels incredibly strong in every direction, almost as strong as steel but a fraction of the weight. Here's the remarkable part: trees absorb CO₂ as they grow, and that carbon stays locked inside the building for its entire lifetime. The Michael Kirby Building's timber structure stores an estimated 1,000+ tonnes of carbon — roughly equivalent to taking 220 cars off the road for a year. Mass timber buildings also go up 30% faster than concrete equivalents and are far quieter to construct — a big advantage on a busy university campus.
